Photography - Manhattan Skyline



Posted on November 6, 2023.

As I begin to settle in with more experience in my film photography, having shot with some inconsistency over the past 3 years, I can see a sense of style and preference begin to emerge in my photography. Shots that capture me; ideas that I feel I’m drawn to. I’ve tried to engage with these shots and have enjoyed that process. I thought about series I’d pursue and images that I’d pair — after a while of shooting the same conceptual thing over, I can feel some monotony affecting me, some stagnant style. I tried to push past that. Beyond the repetition I feel like I’ve found some collection of photos that I’m happy with. Some sort of a A First Collection (capitalized) if you will. Looking at all of these images now together I can even see things in hindsight that I didn’t fully grasp.



This collection began with an interest in the tops of buildings. I liked how there were different styles and embellishments made by the architects and I also liked how they had the sky as a background. The building itself sort of can grow into the frame, like a mountain. This is the subject of my image but I even found that layering behind and on top can lead to great photos. I like when a closer object can exist further in the foreground. Whether another building or a tree, these stacks within the frame can help to add richness via depth. What could be more simple yet so graceful than this? The more sky is involved, the more calm the photo. The more of the building, the more the subject is presented as an entity. The more the obfuscating tree or feature or other buildings, the busier the shot becomes. It’s nothing special. Just a good old fashioned picture of buildings.



At the time I had initially written notes about the photos I was taking, I had thought of them as some sort of “internal skyline”. The skylines of cities being famous and signifying a sense of belonging. People getting posters or paintings or even tattoos of their skylines. I thought of the skyline as someone living actually inside the city as being very different. My New York City skyline are the corners of buildings that I see everyday, that I know well. The traditional NYC Skyline is something awe inspiring, yes, but I can’t see it. I’d have to be in Jersey or Brooklyn.



Another thing that makes this collection special is it clearly shows the difference between my cameras as I move from shooting 35mm on a Holga 120 to a Canon FTB and, only recently, a Ricoh XR-P.
